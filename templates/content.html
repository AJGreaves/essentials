Headings on a web page serve the same purpose as headings in a document, online article or magazine. They are designed to catch your attention, and so they are larger than the main text.

In HTML, headings have six different levels from level 1 - the largest, to level 6 - the smallest. In the past, we were advised to use the headings in order. That is - you shouldn't use a level 2 heading as the main headline on the page. In modern web design, though, we care more about using whichever heading makes sense for the look we're trying to achieve.
